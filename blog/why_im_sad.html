Why I'm Sad

The perfect position that never happened

"you were the first person to solve this entire problem"

"If you decide to come, look me up, I have some questions about your research"

These were phrases from my interviewers after I passed the technical bar for Google Research. I had applied for years, and been rejected equally often. This time, however, I was well prepared and got glowing feedback.

Then came the dreaded project matching phase. I could still end up without a spot, even given the nice words and my performance (foreshadowing).

My recruiter asked if I had reached out to any teams yet. I had. When I mentioned which teams I was talking with, she brought up a small problem.

"That team sits in Paris, your application was only for North America." Oops. I applied again, interviewed again, and again passed the technical bar.

I was ecstatic. I reached out to the team in Paris and we met at NeurIPS 2019. I had an accepted paper in their workshop on optimal transport theory. They seemed impressed with my skills and said they would talk with other people at the conference then submit a project internally.

This morning, almost 5 months after beginning the process, I found out that the single spot on their team has been filled and I wouldn’t be going after all.

I’m disappointed. Mostly at the situation.

My PhD studies are not going as planned. I am stuck and this was a clear way to add gas to my small flame of research talent. I’m not sure what comes next, but I do know something will work out.

My ideal role

Throughout this afternoon and evening, I have had time to reflect on what I truly want to do for work. The generic answers have circled time and again in my mind.

"I want to have an impact"

"I want to leave my mark"

These answers really aren’t helpful. And so my introspective journey continued, round and round. I want to work in ML, using my applied math skills I’ve so carefully honed.

I want to have leadership and responsibility. I want ownership and to create value in an organization. But am I punching above my weight class? I don’t have the deep systemic expertize most groups are looking for in a leader.

The natural question that follows is “how can I gain that skill, while not going crazy from lack of ownership?”

I’m not sure. There may not even be an optimal answer.

I do know I want to keep abreast on modern research, implement prototypes, present my work to decision makers, and guide strategic processes. I know I have a lot to learn, but I also have a large skill set that can be used to generate value.

Next Steps

The truth is, I don’t know what comes next for me. I don’t think finishing my PhD at my current institution is the answer. My needs and goals are taking a back seat in this program.

I guess I’ll officially be a college drop out :)
